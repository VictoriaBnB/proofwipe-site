# AlternativeTo listing for ProofWipe

This is the copy and the plan for listing ProofWipe on AlternativeTo. You submit it yourself (AlternativeTo is a community site, so a first-party submission from you is the right move). Everything you need to paste is below.

## Why bother with AlternativeTo

People who are looking to replace DBAN, Blancco, KillDisk, or Eraser search AlternativeTo directly, and every listing there ranks well on Google for "[product] alternative" searches. It is a free, high-authority backlink and a steady trickle of exactly the buyers you want: someone actively shopping for a wiping tool. It pairs perfectly with the DBAN alternative page already on the site.

## How to submit

Go to alternativeto.net, make a free account, and use "Add application" (top menu, under your profile, or at alternativeto.net/manage/add-item/). Fill in the fields with the copy below. It goes into a moderation queue and usually gets approved within a few days. Once it is live, you can add ProofWipe as an alternative to the competitor listings (see the last section), which is where most of the traffic comes from.

## Fields to paste

Name: ProofWipe

Short tagline (one line): Verified NIST 800-88 drive wiping for Windows, with a tamper-evident certificate for every drive.

Description:
ProofWipe is a Windows tool for securely erasing drives to the NIST 800-88 standard, built for modern SSDs and NVMe drives. It runs a Clear (overwrite) or a Purge (the drive's own firmware sanitize command), reads the drive back to verify the data is actually gone, and produces a tamper-evident certificate for each drive listing the drive, its serial number, the method, the verification result, and the date. Anyone can verify a certificate in their browser at proofwipe.com/verify with nothing uploaded.

It runs as a desktop app for wiping many drives in parallel, and as a bootable USB edition for wiping a machine's own internal drive, including laptops with soldered-on storage. It protects the drive it is running from so you cannot wipe the wrong thing by accident. Pricing is flat with no per-drive fees.

Good fit for IT resellers, refurbishers, MSPs, and any business that has to prove drives were sanitized before disposal.

Official website: https://proofwipe.com

Category / tags: Backup & Sync is wrong; use these: Disk Utility, Secure Erase, Data Destruction, Privacy, System & Hardware. Add tags: nist-800-88, secure-erase, ssd, nvme, data-sanitization, certificate, bootable-usb.

Platforms: Windows. (Also add "Self-Hosted" only if it is offered; otherwise leave Windows alone. The bootable USB edition still runs on Windows/WinPE, so do not tick Linux or Mac.)

Licensing model: Commercial / Paid. (There is a free tier, so also tick "Freemium" if AlternativeTo offers it.)

Pricing: Free tier available, Pro and Business paid tiers, flat pricing with no per-drive fees.

## Screenshots to upload

AlternativeTo listings with images get far more clicks. Upload two or three: the finished "Sanitization Verified" certificate, the method-selection screen with Purge chosen, and the bootable-stick or drive-list view. Use the same clean screenshots you use on the site.

## After it is approved: this is the real traffic

The listing itself is fine, but the traffic comes from being listed as an alternative on the pages people already visit. Once ProofWipe is live, open each of these and use "Suggest as alternative to" (or the "Add" option on the alternatives list):

- DBAN (Darik's Boot and Nuke)
- Blancco Drive Eraser
- KillDisk
- Eraser
- Parted Magic
- CCleaner Drive Wiper
- nwipe

For DBAN especially, add a short "why it is a good alternative" note when prompted, something like: "Handles SSD and NVMe properly with a firmware Purge, verifies the wipe by reading back, and gives you a tamper-evident certificate for each drive, which DBAN does not." That note is what convinces someone scrolling the DBAN alternatives to click through.

## One tip

Keep the tone factual, not salesy. AlternativeTo moderators and the community downrank listings that read like ads. The copy above is deliberately plain and feature-focused for that reason. Let the certificate and the SSD-handling do the selling.
